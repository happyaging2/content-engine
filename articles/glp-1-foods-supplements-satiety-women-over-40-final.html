What to Know

  • GLP-1 is a natural gut hormone produced in your small intestine after eating
  • High-fiber foods, quality protein, and specific supplements may modestly support your body's natural GLP-1 secretion
  • Gut bacteria that ferment fiber produce short-chain fatty acids (butyrate, propionate) that directly stimulate GLP-1 release
  • These dietary strategies are NOT a substitute for GLP-1 medications if your doctor has prescribed them
  • After 40, GLP-1 sensitivity may decline due to hormonal shifts

What Is GLP-1?

GLP-1 is an incretin hormone produced by L-cells in the small intestine and colon in response to eating. It signals satiety, stimulates insulin release, and slows gastric emptying.

Why GLP-1 Matters More After 40

After 40, estrogen decline affects insulin sensitivity and gut microbiome composition. Gut microbiome diversity declines, reducing production of short-chain fatty acids that stimulate GLP-1 release.
