## Checklist Methodology Notes

- **Primary keyword in title:** Checked comment/title tag for primary keyword term.
- **Primary keyword in first 100 words:** Checked first `<p>` body paragraph (excluding the what-to-know box, which is not body copy).
- **What-to-know box:** Checked for `what-to-know` class or "What to Know" heading.
- **5+ H2 sections:** Counted `<h2>` tags in each file.
- **FAQ 4+ questions:** Articles 6 and 7 use a `Q:` style format (`<p><strong>Q:</strong></p>`) rather than `<h3>` FAQ tags; this is acceptable and each has 5 questions. Articles 3, 18, and 19 use `<h3>` FAQ format with 4 questions each -- all meet minimum.
- **References with DOIs:** Required `DOI:` identifiers in the reference list items. Five articles originally used `PMID:` only and were fixed.
- **No em/en dashes:** No occurrences of `—` or `–` found in any article.
- **No ALL CAPS headings:** No all-caps headings found in any article.
- **Product card:** Verified `product-card-inline` class with Shop Now link and price in every article.
- **2+ body images:** Counted Unsplash URLs (product card images are from CDN Shopify and excluded from body image count). Two articles had only 2 Unsplash images and were fixed to 3.

## Observations for Future Batches

1. **Reference format consistency:** Writers using research articles with PMIDs should add DOIs at writing time. DOIs are persistent identifiers and preferred for SEO/GEO citation markup.
2. **Image placement:** Aim for 3 body images per article as a standard, not just the minimum of 2. Ideal placement is after every 2-3 H2 sections.
3. **FAQ format:** Two articles (deep-sleep, perimenopause) used `Q:` format inside `<p>` tags rather than `<h3>` question format. While this meets the count requirement, the `<h3>` format is preferred for SEO structured data markup.
4. **All articles:** No em dashes, en dashes, or ALL CAPS headings were found across all 20 articles -- excellent consistency from the writing team.
