What Is Ritual Multivitamin?

Ritual Essential for Women is a daily multivitamin that covers key micronutrient bases with a focus on bioavailable ingredient forms and supply chain transparency. Ritual's approach to formulation involves using "traceable" ingredients with documented sourcing and prioritizing forms of nutrients that research suggests are better absorbed than conventional supplement forms.

The Essential for Women formulation (there are age-specific versions including one for women 18+, 50+, and a prenatal) typically includes folate as 5-MTHF (methylfolate, the active form), vitamin D3 as cholecalciferol, omega-3 DHA from algae (vegan-friendly), vitamin K2 as MK-7, magnesium from magnesium chelate, boron, and selected B vitamins. The product is designed for the capsule-in-capsule format that allows oil-based and dry ingredients to be separated.

Ritual does not contain iron in most formulas (citing research that many women do not need supplemental iron outside of specific life stages), which is a deliberate formulation decision that some women appreciate and others find limits its utility as a complete one-stop multivitamin. It also does not contain calcium, as Ritual has cited concerns about calcium supplement absorption and interaction with iron absorption in multivitamin format.

What Is Happy Aging NAD+ Women's Longevity Formula?

Happy Aging's NAD+ Women's Longevity Formula is a targeted supplement designed around the biology of aging in women over 40, not nutritional deficiency prevention. The formula centers on NAD+ precursor support and cellular energy optimization, which is a fundamentally different goal than a multivitamin.

Key areas addressed by longevity-focused NAD+ formulas for women include: NAD+ precursor delivery (supporting the restoration of NAD+ levels that decline by roughly 50% between age 40 and 60), mitochondrial support (ingredients that support electron transport chain efficiency and mitochondrial membrane health), sirtuin activation (the NAD+-dependent longevity protein family), adaptogenic support for hormonal resilience during perimenopause, and anti-inflammatory support relevant to the chronic low-grade inflammation that accelerates with menopause.

This is not a product designed to prevent iron or vitamin D deficiency. It is a product designed to address the cellular aging biology that accelerates in women after 40 and is not addressed by micronutrient supplementation alone.

Why It Matters for Women Over 40

The transition into perimenopause and menopause changes what a woman's body most needs from supplementation. In her 20s and 30s, the primary nutritional risks are deficiencies in key micronutrients, folate and iron for reproductive health, vitamin D for immune and bone function, and omega-3 fatty acids for cardiovascular and brain health. A good multivitamin like Ritual addresses these gaps well.

After 40, a new category of biological challenges becomes relevant. NAD+ levels drop significantly, reducing cellular energy production capacity and impairing sirtuin and PARP activity (the DNA repair and stress response proteins that depend on NAD+). Mitochondrial quality begins to decline more noticeably. Chronic low-grade inflammation, sometimes called "inflammaging," becomes a more active factor in tissue aging. Hormonal volatility creates oxidative stress that depletes cellular antioxidant reserves.

A standard multivitamin provides the nutritional building blocks the body needs to function. A longevity formula for women over 40 addresses the cellular mechanisms of aging that begin to actively work against you in this decade, regardless of whether your nutritional status is excellent. These are parallel needs, not competing ones.

What the Research Says

Ritual's evidence base rests primarily on the ingredient-level research for specific micronutrients in their bioavailable forms. The clinical evidence for methylfolate (5-MTHF) over folic acid in women with MTHFR polymorphisms is well established. Vitamin D3 is better supported than D2 for raising serum 25-hydroxyvitamin D. Algae-sourced DHA is a clinically equivalent source to fish-based omega-3 for brain and cardiovascular support. These are legitimate evidence-based formulation choices.

The clinical evidence for NAD+ precursors in women over 40 has grown substantially in recent years. The landmark 2021 study by Yoshino et al. (PMID: 34088354) demonstrated that NMN supplementation improved muscle insulin sensitivity in postmenopausal women with prediabetes. Research on mitochondrial support compounds and their effects on cellular energy in midlife women continues to grow, establishing a genuine evidence base for longevity-targeted formulation beyond simple micronutrient provision.

No head-to-head randomized trial has compared a multivitamin to a longevity formula specifically in women over 40, but this is expected: they address different biological targets, so a direct comparison would not be meaningful from a trial design perspective.

What the Evidence Does Not Support

Claims that either product category alone is sufficient for comprehensive women's health support over 40 exceed what the evidence supports.

A multivitamin cannot address NAD+ decline. This is simply not a target within the micronutrient framework. No combination of B vitamins, vitamin D, and omega-3 will restore the NAD+ levels that decline with age, because NAD+ decline is driven by enzyme activity changes and increased NAD+ consumption (by CD38 and PARP enzymes) that are not corrected by micronutrient repletion.

Conversely, an NAD+ longevity formula cannot replace a multivitamin. Women who are deficient in vitamin D, are not getting adequate omega-3 from diet, or have suboptimal folate status will not have those gaps addressed by a NAD+ formula. These are genuinely different nutritional territories.

Neither product is a substitute for lifestyle. Neither Ritual nor Happy Aging's formula compensates for poor sleep, sedentary behavior, or a diet that is consistently deficient in whole foods. Supplements fill gaps and support optimal function; they do not create it from scratch.

Who This Is NOT For

Happy Aging's NAD+ Women's Longevity Formula is not for women who are still in the process of establishing basic nutritional foundations. If you are not consistently sleeping, if your diet is highly processed, if you are not getting adequate sunlight or dietary vitamin D, address those foundations first. A longevity formula built on a poor nutritional and lifestyle base will underperform.

Women who need comprehensive micronutrient coverage, particularly if they follow a vegan diet, have absorption issues, or have known deficiency risks, should prioritize a quality multivitamin over a longevity formula as their first supplement investment. Ritual is a sensible choice in this category.

Women who are pregnant, trying to conceive, or breastfeeding have different nutritional priorities entirely. The prenatal period has specific requirements for folate, iron, DHA, and iodine that neither of these general-population products is designed to meet. A prenatal multivitamin formulated for this specific life stage is the appropriate choice during pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes. These products serve different purposes and do not have ingredient overlap that would create toxicity risk at their respective standard doses. If you are uncertain about specific combinations given your health history, a brief conversation with your physician or pharmacist is a reasonable step.

Is Ritual worth it compared to cheaper multivitamins?

Ritual charges a premium for its formulation philosophy: bioavailable ingredient forms, supply chain transparency, and clean additives. Whether this premium is worth it depends on your priorities. The ingredient form choices Ritual makes (methylfolate over folic acid, D3 over D2, algae DHA) are evidence-informed and meaningful for a subset of women, particularly those with MTHFR polymorphisms or who prefer vegan-sourced omega-3. The B vitamins in Ritual play a role in the broad metabolic pathways where NAD+ operates, but B vitamin supplementation does not meaningfully raise NAD+ levels in adults who are not severely deficient. Ritual is not designed to be a longevity formula.
