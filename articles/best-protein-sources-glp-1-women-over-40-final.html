Best Protein Sources for Women on GLP-1 Medications After 40

Women on GLP-1 medications like semaglutide or tirzepatide need at least 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight per day to protect muscle mass during weight loss, but appetite suppression often makes hitting that target through food alone nearly impossible.

What to Know Before You Read
  • GLP-1 medications suppress appetite dramatically, making protein targets very difficult to hit through food alone.
  • Recommended protein intake: 1.2 to 1.6 g per kg of body weight per day.
  • Leucine content is the most important protein quality metric. High-leucine proteins trigger muscle protein synthesis most effectively.
  • Some high-fat protein sources may slow gastric emptying further and worsen GLP-1 side effects like nausea.
  • Protein supplementation is frequently necessary on GLP-1 therapy, not a luxury.
  • Timing matters: spreading protein across 3 to 4 meals activates muscle protein synthesis more effectively than one large serving.

What the Research Says

A 2023 analysis published in Obesity Reviews found that spontaneous protein intake dropped significantly alongside overall calorie reduction in GLP-1-treated adults. Structured protein supplementation was associated with better lean mass preservation outcomes.

According to Happy Aging's review of protein research in GLP-1 users over 40, consistent distribution of protein across the day combined with resistance training is the single most effective muscle-preservation strategy available.

The 5-Step Daily Protein Plan

  1. Calculate your target: Multiply your body weight in kg by 1.4 for a middle-of-range target.
  2. Distribute across 3 to 4 meals. Aim for 25 to 35 grams per meal.
  3. Start with protein at every meal. When appetite is low, eat protein first.
  4. Add one protein supplement daily. One serving of quality lean protein (25-30g) covers 25-30% of most women's daily targets.
  5. Pair with resistance training 2 to 3 times per week. Resistance training amplifies the value of every gram of protein you eat.
