What Is NAD+ and Why Do These Products Exist?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It powers energy production in the mitochondria, activates proteins called sirtuins that regulate aging, and is essential for DNA repair. Without adequate NAD+, cells lose their ability to function efficiently.

For women over 40, this matters because NAD+ levels decline significantly with age. Research published in Cell by Gomes et al. (PMID 23140366) documented the cascade of cellular dysfunction that follows NAD+ depletion, including mitochondrial failure and accelerated aging markers. The goal of NAD+ supplements is to restore or maintain levels that naturally fall as you age.

What the Research Says

The research base for nicotinamide riboside (NR), the active ingredient in Life Extension NAD+ Cell Regenerator, is genuinely solid. Dellinger et al. conducted a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in 120 healthy adults showing that NR supplementation at 1000mg/day safely raised blood NAD+ levels by up to 2.7-fold compared to placebo (PMID 29862351). This is real, human, peer-reviewed data.

However, there is an important limitation: that trial was not conducted in women over 40 experiencing hormonal shifts, and it used a higher dose than the 300mg in Life Extension's product. The jump from trial data to real-world dosing is not always straightforward.

A separate but related mechanism involves an enzyme called CD38, which degrades NAD+ at an accelerating rate as people age. Camacho-Pereira et al. (PMID 27304503) demonstrated that CD38 activity is a major driver of age-related NAD+ decline, separate from precursor availability. A formula that only adds a precursor does not address CD38 activity. NAD Advanced includes ingredients targeting this pathway as part of its multi-blend design.

According to Happy Aging's review of the available literature through 2026, the strongest outcomes for women over 40 come from approaches that combine NAD+ precursors with CD38 inhibition, mitochondrial support, and cellular cleanup mechanisms.
