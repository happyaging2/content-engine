Marine Collagen vs Bovine Collagen for Skin After 40: Which Is Better?

For women over 40 focused specifically on skin health, marine collagen is the better-researched and likely more bioavailable option. Both marine and bovine collagen have human trial evidence supporting improvements in skin hydration and elasticity, but marine collagen's smaller peptide size, predominantly Type I composition, and growing body of RCT data make it a stronger choice when skin is the primary goal.

What to Know Before You Read
  • For skin, Type I collagen is the most important. It makes up roughly 80-90% of the collagen in skin.
  • Marine collagen comes from fish skin and scales. It is almost entirely Type I collagen.
  • Bovine collagen comes from cow hides and connective tissue. It provides primarily Type I and Type III collagen.
  • Smaller peptide size may improve absorption. Marine collagen peptides are generally smaller than bovine.
  • Both sources have RCT evidence for skin outcomes.
  • Collagen production declines by approximately 1-1.5% per year after age 25, accelerating around menopause.

What the Research Says

A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published in the Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology found that women taking 2.5 grams of hydrolyzed marine collagen peptides daily for 12 weeks showed approximately 28% improvement in skin hydration versus 9% in the placebo group. A bovine collagen RCT at 10 grams per day for 8 weeks showed significant improvements in skin elasticity and reduction in periorbital wrinkle depth.

According to Happy Aging's review, the evidence consistently supports marine collagen as the better-targeted choice for skin outcomes, though both sources provide genuine benefit at adequate doses.

The Happy Aging Recommendation

  1. Choose a fully hydrolyzed marine collagen product (low molecular weight, typically under 3,000 daltons).
  2. Use 2.5 to 10 grams per day. Lower doses in the 2.5-5 gram range appear effective for skin specifically.
  3. Take consistently for at least 8 to 12 weeks.
  4. Pair with adequate vitamin C (75-120 mg daily), which is required for collagen synthesis.
  5. Protect skin from UV exposure. SPF 30 or higher daily is the most evidence-supported skin health intervention.
