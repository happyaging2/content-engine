How Declining NAD+ Affects Metabolism and Weight After 40

Declining NAD+ levels after 40 reduce the activity of key metabolic enzymes, including sirtuins and AMPK, which makes the body less efficient at burning fat and managing blood sugar. This is a real biological mechanism behind the experience many women have of their diet and exercise routine not working the same after 40.

What to Know Before You Read
  • NAD+ is a coenzyme required for energy metabolism in every cell. It activates sirtuins and AMPK, enzymes that regulate fat burning and glucose use.
  • NAD+ levels drop by roughly 50% between your 20s and 50s due to age-related changes in synthesis and accelerated degradation by CD38.
  • Lower NAD+ means less mitochondrial efficiency, reduced fat oxidation, and impaired metabolic signaling.
  • Human trials show NMN supplementation can improve muscle insulin sensitivity in women with prediabetes (PMID 33009199).
  • NAD+ precursors are not weight loss supplements. They support the metabolic pathways that diet and exercise also require.

Why NAD+ Runs Your Metabolism

NAD+ serves as a co-factor for sirtuins (SIRT1, SIRT3, SIRT6), which regulate fat oxidation, mitochondrial biogenesis, and glucose metabolism. PARP enzymes that repair DNA also consume NAD+. CD38, which increases with inflammation and aging, breaks down NAD+ aggressively. Less NAD+ means less sirtuin activity, less efficient fat oxidation, impaired mitochondrial biogenesis, and reduced AMPK signaling.

What the Research Says

Yoshino et al. 2021 (PMID 33009199), published in Science, enrolled 25 postmenopausal women with prediabetes and gave half 250 mg NMN daily for 10 weeks. The NMN group showed significantly improved skeletal muscle insulin sensitivity and changes in muscle gene expression consistent with improved mitochondrial function.

According to Happy Aging's review of the published research, the evidence supports NAD+ restoration as a real metabolic intervention with measurable effects on insulin sensitivity in women. It does not support supplementation as a standalone solution for weight management.
