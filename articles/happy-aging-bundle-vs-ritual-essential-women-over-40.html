Why It Matters for Women Over 40

The biological changes that begin in your 40s are driven by mechanisms that a multivitamin cannot address. NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme essential for cellular energy production, DNA repair, and the activity of sirtuins (a family of proteins associated with longevity regulation). Research suggests NAD+ levels decline by roughly 50% between ages 40 and 60, a drop that is not caused by micronutrient deficiency and cannot be corrected by standard multivitamin supplementation. Similarly, the collagen loss associated with estrogen decline after 40 requires specific collagen peptides to support, not vitamin C alone. Sleep architecture changes with age in ways that affect cellular repair, and targeted sleep support addresses mechanisms that no multivitamin ingredient meaningfully influences.

What the Research Says

The research case for NMN and NAD+ precursors has grown substantially. A 2022 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled multicenter trial (PMC: PMC9261366, PMID: 35821806) tested NMN supplementation (150 mg twice daily) in 66 healthy adults aged 40-65 over 60 days. The trial found significant increases in blood NAD+/NADH levels at both day 30 and day 60, with no significant adverse effects. A larger dose-dependent trial published the same year (PMID: 36482258) found that 300 mg, 600 mg, and 900 mg daily doses all significantly increased NAD+ levels, with the 600 mg and 900 mg doses also improving scores on physical function assessments (six-minute walk test).

Marine collagen peptides have multiple RCTs supporting effects on skin elasticity, hydration, and wrinkle depth in women over 40, including the Proksch et al. 2014 trials and Asserin et al. 2015. These are not outcomes that any standard multivitamin ingredient produces.

For Ritual specifically, the evidence base is strongest for the individual ingredients (D3, methylated B12, omega-3 DHA) rather than for the specific formula, which is true of most multivitamins. The micronutrients Ritual includes are well-supported individually; the question is whether healthy women eating a reasonable diet have the gaps these nutrients fill.

What the Evidence Doesn't Support

Neither product should be positioned as a complete solution to aging. No supplement stack reverses biological aging, extends lifespan with certainty, or replaces the foundational lifestyle factors that drive long-term health: consistent exercise (especially resistance training), quality sleep, a varied whole-food diet, stress management, and regular health screening. Supplements work within that context, not instead of it.

For Ritual specifically, the case for multivitamins in generally healthy adults who eat a varied diet is weaker than supplement marketing suggests. Large trials including the Physicians' Health Study II and components of the VITAL study have not shown dramatic benefits of multivitamin supplementation on major health outcomes in well-nourished populations. This does not mean multivitamins are useless, especially for women with specific dietary patterns, absorption issues, or life circumstances that create genuine gaps. It means the "take your multivitamin for health insurance" premise is less evidence-backed than commonly assumed.

For NAD+ precursor supplements including those in the Happy Aging bundle, the human trial evidence is growing but still relatively young. We have good mechanistic evidence and short-term trials showing NAD+ restoration in humans. The translation of increased NAD+ levels to long-term health outcomes (reduced disease risk, meaningful improvements in aging trajectories) is biologically plausible and supported by animal research but not yet fully confirmed in decades-long human trials. That is an honest caveat worth knowing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take Ritual and the Happy Aging Bundle at the same time?

In principle, yes, because they target different biological mechanisms and have minimal ingredient overlap. The main consideration is avoiding excessive amounts of any single nutrient (particularly fat-soluble vitamins D and K2, which both products may contain). Review the combined totals against safe upper intake levels, and consider discussing the combined stack with your healthcare provider if you are on any medications or have specific health conditions.

What makes NAD+ supplementation different from a standard B vitamin complex?

NAD+ precursors like NMN and NR are specifically designed to raise intracellular NAD+ levels, which drives cellular energy production, DNA repair, and sirtuin activation. Standard B vitamins (including niacin and niacinamide, which are B3) do contribute to NAD+ synthesis but through different pathways and at different efficiencies. High-dose niacin in particular has well-documented side effects (flushing) at the doses needed to meaningfully raise NAD+. NMN and NR are specifically developed to raise NAD+ more efficiently and with a better side effect profile, which is why they are not interchangeable with standard B complex vitamins for longevity applications.

Is Ritual's traceable sourcing model meaningful?

Yes, it is a genuine differentiator. The supplement industry has well-documented quality control problems: mislabeled ingredients, contamination, and ingredients that do not match their label claims are real issues. Ritual's supply chain transparency and third-party testing model addresses these concerns in a way that most multivitamin brands do not. For women who care about what they are actually putting in their body, this matters.

How long does it take to notice benefits from the Happy Aging Complete Bundle?

Different components work on different timelines. Sleep support may produce noticeable changes within the first week for some women. Energy and mental clarity from NAD+ support is often noticed within 4-8 weeks. Skin changes from marine collagen typically require 8-12 weeks of consistent daily use. The longevity-related cellular effects (DNA repair support, sirtuin activity) operate on longer timelines that are not directly perceptible but accumulate over months and years of consistent use.

Does the research on NMN and longevity in animals apply to humans?

Animal research on NAD+ precursors and longevity is compelling and has driven significant interest in human trials. Multiple human trials have now confirmed that NMN and NR supplementation raises NAD+ levels in humans. Whether that NAD+ restoration translates to the dramatic lifespan extension seen in some animal models is not yet established in human trials of the necessary duration. The current human evidence supports improved energy, physical function, and physiological markers associated with healthy aging, which is a meaningful but more measured claim than "extends lifespan."
