What Is a Longevity Reset?

A longevity reset is a structured, time-bounded protocol designed to establish the foundational behaviors and inputs that longevity science identifies as most impactful for biological aging in a specific population. For women over 40, this means addressing the specific physiological changes that accumulate through perimenopause and beyond: declining NAD+ levels affecting cellular energy, sleep architecture changes from hormonal shifts, gut microbiome alterations that affect immunity and mood, elevated baseline stress and cortisol, and loss of muscle mass from declining activity and protein synthesis efficiency.

The "reset" framing is intentional. Thirty days is long enough to establish new patterns at the cellular, microbiome, and behavioral level, and short enough to stay engaged with a defined endpoint. It is a commitment with a measurable beginning and middle, not an open-ended lifestyle overhaul that asks for indefinite willpower.

Why This Matters After 40

The women's longevity research of the past decade has moved from a disease-prevention model toward a vitality-optimization model. The question is no longer just "how do we prevent illness?" but "how do we maintain and even improve function, energy, cognitive clarity, and physical resilience through and beyond menopause?" The answer emerging from research involves multiple simultaneous interventions rather than single-target approaches.

The Five Longevity Pillars

Each pillar is supported by its own body of evidence and addresses a distinct physiological system. The synergy between pillars is what makes simultaneous implementation more powerful than sequential addressing of each.

Pillar 1: Cellular Energy (NAD+). NAD+ is a coenzyme central to mitochondrial function, DNA repair, and cellular stress response. Research indicates NAD+ levels decline with age, beginning meaningfully in the mid-30s and accelerating through the 40s and 50s. Rajman, Chwalek, and Sinclair (2018, Cell Metabolism, PMID 29502867) reviewed the in vivo evidence for NAD+-boosting molecules, finding that NAD+ precursor supplementation supports cellular energy metabolism and activates longevity-associated pathways including sirtuins. Yoshino et al. (2021, Science, PMID 34811282) conducted a randomized controlled trial of NMN supplementation in 25 prediabetic women, finding improvements in muscle insulin sensitivity over 10 weeks of 300mg per day dosing.

Pillar 2: Sleep Quality. Sleep is when cellular repair, memory consolidation, hormonal regulation, and immune function are most active. Poor sleep in women over 40, driven largely by estrogen and progesterone decline affecting sleep architecture, accelerates multiple aging pathways simultaneously. Addressing sleep is not optional in a longevity protocol because poor sleep blunts the benefits of every other pillar. A randomized controlled trial (Abbasi et al., 2012, PMID 23853635) found magnesium supplementation improved sleep efficiency by 13.6% versus placebo in 46 older adults, providing a practical and accessible entry point for sleep support.

Pillar 3: Stress Regulation. Chronic psychological and physiological stress elevates cortisol, which suppresses NAD+ utilization, disrupts sleep, promotes inflammatory signaling, impairs gut barrier function, and directly accelerates cellular aging. Stress regulation is the pillar that women over 40 most commonly deprioritize, partly because the strategies (meditation, breathing, nature exposure) feel less "medical" than supplements or exercise. The evidence, however, is consistent: regular stress-reduction practices reduce cortisol and inflammatory markers in ways that directly support the other four pillars.

Pillar 4: Gut Health. The gut microbiome shifts significantly through menopause, with reduced diversity and loss of key bacteria affecting immune regulation, mood neurotransmitter production, estrogen metabolism, and inflammatory tone. Addressing gut health through dietary fiber, fermented foods, and targeted probiotic support directly supports immune resilience and the microbial environment that influences every other pillar.

Pillar 5: Movement. Regular physical activity is the most consistently evidence-supported longevity intervention across all populations. For women over 40, two movement types are most critical: Zone 2 cardio (low-intensity sustained aerobic exercise at approximately 60-70% maximum heart rate) for mitochondrial health and metabolic efficiency, and resistance training for muscle mass preservation and bone density. Zone 2 cardio in particular supports mitochondrial biogenesis, which directly complements NAD+ supplementation at the cellular energy pillar.

The 30-Day Longevity Reset Protocol

Week 1: Foundation

  1. Establish a fixed wake time. Choose a realistic wake time and commit to it for 7 days, including weekends. This anchors the circadian rhythm and initiates improvements to sleep architecture even before other interventions are in place.
  2. Start NAD+ supplementation. Begin NAD Advanced Longevity Formula with breakfast each morning. Include some dietary fat in your breakfast to support absorption of fat-soluble cofactors.
  3. Set a protein target of 25-30g per meal. This supports muscle protein synthesis, satiety, and metabolic efficiency. Concrete sources: 3 eggs (18g protein) with Greek yogurt (10-15g) at breakfast; a palm-sized portion of chicken, fish, or legumes at lunch and dinner. Track for one week to calibrate your baseline.
  4. Begin Zone 2 cardio 3 times this week. Zone 2 is low-intensity sustained cardio at approximately 60-70% of your maximum heart rate. You should be able to hold a conversation. Duration: 25-30 minutes per session. Walking briskly, cycling, swimming, or light jogging all qualify. This level of intensity specifically supports mitochondrial biogenesis, which complements NAD+ supplementation.
  5. Start magnesium glycinate before bed. Take 300-400mg approximately 1 hour before your target sleep time. This supports GABA receptor activity and addresses the progesterone-decline mechanism behind middle-of-the-night waking. Give this at least 5-7 days before evaluating.
  6. Take a 10-minute post-dinner walk each evening. This improves gastric emptying, reduces evening blood sugar spikes, and lowers cortisol before sleep. It is also a natural transition signal that the active part of your day is ending.
  7. End of Week 1 check-in: Note your baseline energy (1-10), sleep quality (1-10), and any digestive symptoms. These are your starting benchmarks.

Week 2: Gut Reset

  1. Eliminate the top 3 inflammatory inputs for 14 days. The three with the most consistent evidence for disrupting microbiome diversity, sleep quality, and inflammatory tone: alcohol (suppress completely for 2 weeks), ultra-processed foods (anything with more than 5 ingredients you cannot identify), and refined sugar outside of whole fruit. This is not permanent restriction; it is a 2-week diagnostic and reset window.
  2. Target 25g or more of dietary fiber per day. Add one fiber-rich food per meal: oats or chia at breakfast (5-8g), large salad with legumes at lunch (10-15g), cooked vegetables and lentils or beans at dinner (8-12g). Track fiber for 3-4 days until you have an accurate sense of your intake.
  3. Start the Happiest Gut probiotic 15-30 minutes before breakfast. This timing maximizes bacterial survival through stomach acid. Expect some gas or mild bloating in the first 5-7 days as the microbiome adapts; this is normal and typically resolves.
  4. Add 2 servings of fermented foods this week. Plain full-fat yogurt with live cultures, kefir, kimchi, or sauerkraut. These provide live bacterial strains in a food matrix that complements supplement-based probiotics.
  5. Track energy and sleep quality daily. By the end of week 2, most women notice the clearest signal from sleep improvement (magnesium glycinate kicking in) and early energy improvements from NAD+ supplementation. Gut changes are longer cycle and will be more apparent in weeks 3-4.

Week 3: Cellular Support

  1. Add a daily stress-reduction practice for the remaining 14 days of the protocol. Choose one: 10-15 minutes of meditation (apps like Headspace or Insight Timer), 5-10 minutes of extended-exhale breathing (5 seconds in, 7 seconds out), or a solo 20-minute nature walk without your phone. Consistency matters more than duration. Even 10 minutes daily produces measurable cortisol reduction over 3-4 weeks.
  2. Optimize supplement timing using the morning and evening structure. Morning with breakfast: NAD Advanced formula, Vitamin D3, CoQ10 if using. Probiotic 15-30 minutes before breakfast. Evening: magnesium glycinate 1 hour before bed, low-dose melatonin 30-60 minutes before sleep. This week is about tightening the protocol you already started, not adding new supplements.
  3. Add resistance training twice this week. Bodyweight exercises at home are sufficient: squats, lunges, push-ups (modified is fine), and a plank hold. Two 20-25 minute sessions. Resistance training and Zone 2 cardio in the same week address different aspects of metabolic health and both are needed for full longevity benefit.
  4. Sleep environment audit. Confirm bedroom temperature is 65-68 degrees Fahrenheit. Remove or cover any blue-light sources. Assess light exposure in the morning: are you getting 10-30 minutes of bright light within an hour of waking? If not, add a morning outdoor walk or a light therapy lamp.
  5. End of Week 3 check-in: Note energy, sleep quality, digestive comfort, and mood stability. Compare to Week 1 baseline. For most women following the protocol with reasonable adherence, energy and sleep scores improve meaningfully at this point.

Week 4: Integration and Assessment

  1. Add one new longevity habit. Choose something you have been considering but have not yet started: a sauna session if available (research on cardiovascular and relaxation benefits, published in Mayo Clinic Proceedings, PMID 29936521), cold water exposure, a new resistance training move, or a weekly meal prep practice. One addition, not several. This week is about building forward momentum, not overloading.
  2. Conduct a full protocol review. Which habits are now automatic? Which still require conscious effort? The automatic habits are wins to protect. The habits still requiring effort need system support: scheduling, accountability, or simplification. Do not abandon practices that feel hard; redesign the system around them.
  3. Review energy, sleep, mood, and digestive function against Week 1 benchmarks. Document specific changes. Common observations at day 28-30: notably more consistent morning energy, fewer middle-of-the-night wakings, reduced post-meal bloating, and improved mood stability during high-stress situations. Some women also notice improvements in skin appearance and body composition from the combined protein, movement, and sleep optimization.
  4. Plan your Month 2 protocol. The 30-day reset is a launching pad, not a destination. Month 2 should retain all five pillars and add one enhancement: increasing Zone 2 cardio duration to 40 minutes, adding a second resistance training session, or upgrading dietary quality in a specific area. Longevity outcomes compound over months and years, not days.

What the Evidence Doesn't Support

The evidence does not support the idea that any single supplement, including NAD+ precursors, can produce longevity benefits equivalent to the combined protocol. NMN and NAD+ precursors are meaningful tools for cellular energy support, but they do not replace sleep, movement, stress regulation, or dietary quality. The research on NAD+ is most compelling when these other factors are also in place, because NAD+ utilization depends on mitochondrial health, which depends on sleep and exercise, and on reduced inflammatory load, which depends on gut health and stress regulation.

Extreme caloric restriction and prolonged fasting protocols, while supported by some evidence in animal models, are not appropriate as primary longevity interventions for perimenopausal and postmenopausal women. The muscle mass implications of caloric restriction in this population require careful medical supervision. Time-restricted eating (a 12-14 hour overnight fast) has a more appropriate evidence base for this group and is integrated into the protocol naturally by establishing consistent meal timing.

No supplement replaces the movement pillar. The mitochondrial benefits of Zone 2 cardio are not replicable by any currently available supplement. NMN and NAD+ precursors support the cellular machinery that exercise trains; they do not substitute for the training itself.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this 30-day plan different from a typical wellness challenge?

Most wellness challenges focus on a single pillar: 30 days of clean eating, or 30 days of meditation, or a supplement trial. This protocol is structured around five simultaneous pillars because the evidence in women's longevity research points to synergistic outcomes from combined approaches. Better sleep improves exercise performance. Better gut health improves mood and stress resilience. NAD+ support improves mitochondrial capacity that exercise can then train. Single-pillar approaches produce results; multi-pillar approaches produce compounding results.

What results can I realistically expect in 30 days?

The most consistent outcomes reported by women following this framework are: improved morning energy by weeks 2-3, fewer middle-of-the-night wakings by weeks 1-2 (from magnesium glycinate), reduced post-meal bloating by weeks 2-3, and improved mood stability by weeks 3-4. Body composition and metabolic changes are longer-cycle and more apparent at 60-90 days. Not every woman will experience every benefit; individual variation in microbiome composition, hormonal status, and baseline habits creates meaningful differences in response timelines.

Can I do this protocol if I am taking prescription medications?

The behavioral components of this protocol (sleep hygiene, Zone 2 cardio, dietary fiber, stress reduction) are appropriate for most women regardless of medication status. For supplement components, consult your pharmacist or physician before adding NMN, magnesium, or probiotics if you take prescription medications, particularly thyroid medications (magnesium can affect levothyroxine absorption timing), blood thinners (fish oil and CoQ10 interactions), or immunosuppressants (probiotics require medical supervision). Pharmacist review of your full supplement list is the most practical first step.

What is Zone 2 cardio and why does it matter for longevity?

Zone 2 cardio is low-intensity sustained aerobic exercise, approximately 60-70% of maximum heart rate, at which you can hold a broken conversation. This intensity specifically stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis, the creation of new mitochondria in muscle cells, more effectively than higher-intensity exercise. Since NAD+ is central to mitochondrial function, Zone 2 cardio and NAD+ supplementation work synergistically. For most women over 40, brisk walking (where talking is possible but requires a little effort) qualifies as Zone 2 without any equipment or gym access.

Do I need all five supplements in the protocol?

The protocol uses NAD Advanced Longevity Formula (cellular energy pillar), Happiest Gut (gut health pillar), and Sleep Tonic or magnesium glycinate (sleep pillar) as the supplement anchors. The remaining two pillars (stress regulation and movement) are behavior-based rather than supplement-based. You can start with one or two supplement pillars and add others progressively. The most impactful starting pairing for most women is NAD Advanced (morning) and magnesium glycinate (evening), as these address the two pillars where women over 40 most consistently report measurable improvement.

Is this protocol safe during perimenopause?

Yes. The protocol is specifically designed for women in and around the perimenopause transition. The sleep support addresses the progesterone-GABA mechanism directly. The gut reset supports the microbiome changes that perimenopause induces. NAD+ support addresses the cellular energy decline that accelerates in midlife. The stress regulation pillar addresses the cortisol dysregulation that compounds hormonal volatility during perimenopause. If you are experiencing severe perimenopausal symptoms including debilitating hot flashes, significant mood disruption, or severe sleep impairment, discuss hormone therapy options with a menopause-literate physician alongside this protocol rather than instead of medical consultation.
