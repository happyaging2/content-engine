Phosphatidylserine for Memory and Focus in Women Over 40: An Evidence-Based Guide

Phosphatidylserine (PS) is one of the few supplements with a qualified health claim from the FDA for cognitive function, and the research behind it is more solid than most nootropics on the market. Multiple randomized controlled trials have examined PS in adults with age-related memory decline, with consistent findings of modest but meaningful improvements in memory recall and processing speed.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Phosphatidylserine is a phospholipid that makes up roughly 15% of the brain's cell membranes.
  • The FDA has granted a qualified health claim linking PS to reduced risk of cognitive decline.
  • Most positive clinical trials used 300 mg of PS daily for 6 to 12 weeks.
  • Sunflower-derived PS is now available for those avoiding soy.
  • PS does not treat dementia. The evidence is strongest for age-related memory decline in otherwise healthy adults.

What the Research Says

A landmark double-blind RCT published in Neurology (1991) by Crook et al. enrolled 149 adults with age-associated memory impairment. At 300 mg of soy-derived PS daily for 12 weeks, verbal recall, learning rate, and overall memory performance improved significantly compared to placebo.

For cortisol modulation, a study published in the European Journal of Clinical Nutrition (1992) by Monteleone et al. found that 800 mg of PS daily significantly blunted cortisol and ACTH response to physical stress in healthy men.

According to Happy Aging's review of the PS literature, the most consistent signals are modest improvement in memory recall in adults with age-related cognitive concerns, and meaningful reduction in stress-induced cortisol response at 300 to 800 mg per day.

The Happy Aging Recommendation

  1. Choose a standardized product with PS listed by milligrams. Effective doses range from 100 to 300 mg per day.
  2. Take it with a meal. PS is fat-soluble and absorbs better with dietary fat.
  3. Be consistent for at least 8 weeks before evaluating results.
  4. Pair with omega-3s if possible. DHA supports the same cell membrane dynamics as PS.
  5. Manage stress alongside supplementation.
