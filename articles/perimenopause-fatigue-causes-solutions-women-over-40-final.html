Perimenopause Fatigue: Why You Are Exhausted and What Actually Helps

Perimenopause fatigue is not just tiredness. It is a distinct kind of exhaustion that does not get better with more sleep, and it has four specific biological causes that most treatment approaches completely miss.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Perimenopause fatigue has four distinct biological causes, not one.
  • Declining progesterone disrupts sleep quality and reduces the brain's calming signals.
  • Estrogen fluctuations directly affect mitochondrial function and cellular energy production.
  • NAD+ decline accelerates after 40, reducing ATP production in every cell.
  • Cortisol dysregulation creates an adrenal load that exhausts the body's stress-response system.
  • Coffee is not a solution. It raises cortisol and deepens the fatigue cycle.

What the Research Says

NAD+ decline in aging tissues is well established. Gomes et al. (2013, PMID 23140366) showed declining NAD+ causes mitochondrial dysfunction and pseudohypoxia in aging muscle cells. Lopez-Otin et al. (2023, PMID 36599349) identified mitochondrial dysfunction as a hallmark of aging with NAD+ decline as a primary driver.

According to Happy Aging's review, no single study addresses all four causes simultaneously. The most effective clinical approaches combine NAD+ support, sleep optimization targeting the progesterone pathway, and adaptogen or magnesium supplementation for cortisol, alongside behavioral foundations.

The Happy Aging Recommendation: 5-Step Morning Energy Protocol

  1. Get outdoor light within 15 minutes of waking. A 10-15 minute walk outside anchors the cortisol awakening response.
  2. Delay caffeine until 90 minutes after waking. The natural cortisol awakening response peaks 30-60 minutes after waking. Waiting 90 minutes lets it complete.
  3. Eat a protein-first breakfast within 60 minutes of waking. Aim for 25-30 grams of protein to stabilize blood sugar and prevent cortisol spikes.
  4. Take NAD+ precursor support with your morning meal. NMN or NR supports the mitochondrial and sirtuin pathways most active earlier in the day.
  5. Do 15-20 minutes of resistance movement before 10 AM if possible. Resistance exercise stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis and increases AMPK signaling.
