Why It Matters for Women Over 40

Collagen loss accelerates in the years around menopause. Type I collagen is the most abundant structural collagen in the skin. Marine collagen is more concentrated in type I than bovine collagen.

Recent research through 2026 continues to support hydrolyzed collagen peptides for skin outcomes in women in the 40-65 age range, with evidence most consistent for daily doses of 2.5-10 grams taken over 8-12 weeks or longer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is marine collagen better absorbed than bovine collagen?

A: Marine collagen peptides have a lower molecular weight than bovine peptides, which means they are smaller and can be absorbed more quickly. The pharmacokinetic difference is real.

Q: Will either of these products help with hair or nails?

A: Type I collagen is found in hair and nails as well as skin. Both products may support hair thickness and nail strength. The clinical evidence for hair and nail outcomes is less robust than for skin but is directionally consistent in smaller studies.
