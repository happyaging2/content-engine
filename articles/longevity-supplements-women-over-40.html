Autophagy: Your Cells' Cleanup System and How to Support It

Autophagy is the process by which cells identify and break down damaged proteins and dysfunctional organelles. After 40, autophagy activity declines, and cellular damage accumulates faster than it can be cleared. This accumulation is now understood to be one of the primary drivers of age-related decline in cellular function. The good news is that autophagy is highly responsive to lifestyle inputs: intermittent fasting (particularly a 16-hour fast), high-intensity exercise, and specific compounds including spermidine, resveratrol, and NAD+ precursors like NMN all activate autophagy through overlapping but distinct pathways. Happy Aging's position: a protocol combining 16:8 intermittent fasting with NMN supplementation addresses both the NAD+-dependent and mTOR-inhibition routes to autophagy activation — the two most important entry points supported by current evidence. Telomeres: What They Are and Why They Matter After 40

Telomeres are the protective caps at the ends of chromosomes — think of them as the plastic tips on shoelaces that prevent fraying. Every time a cell divides, telomeres shorten slightly. When telomeres become critically short, cells enter a state called senescence (they stop dividing and become inflammatory) or die. Telomere shortening is a measurable biological clock, and rates of shortening are significantly influenced by lifestyle: chronic stress, poor sleep, smoking, and processed food accelerate shortening, while consistent exercise, good sleep, and diets high in antioxidants slow it. According to Happy Aging's review of the telomere intervention literature, the most robust lifestyle modifiers of telomere length in women over 40 are aerobic exercise (particularly moderate-intensity, consistent frequency) and stress reduction practices. No supplement has been shown to reliably lengthen telomeres in humans, but several slow the rate of shortening. For a complete evidence review, read our guide to telomere length after 40.

Urolithin A: The Mitophagy Molecule From Pomegranates

Urolithin A is a compound produced by gut bacteria from ellagitannins found in pomegranates, walnuts, and raspberries. Its primary mechanism of action is mitophagy — the selective autophagy of damaged mitochondria. Healthy mitochondria are the energy factories of your cells; as damaged ones accumulate with age, cellular energy output falls. Urolithin A clears damaged mitochondria and stimulates the production of new, functional replacements, effectively renewing the mitochondrial pool. Human clinical trials have found that urolithin A supplementation improves muscle strength and endurance in older adults, with measurable improvements in mitochondrial markers. The catch is that only about 40% of people produce urolithin A efficiently from food due to differences in gut bacteria composition, making supplementation the most reliable delivery method. Sirtuins: The NAD+-Powered Longevity Proteins

Sirtuins are a family of seven enzymes that regulate gene expression, DNA repair, inflammation control, and metabolic efficiency. They require NAD+ as a cofactor to function, which means their activity declines in parallel with NAD+ as you age. Reactivating sirtuins through NAD+ restoration is one of the most studied and best-supported longevity strategies. SIRT1, the most extensively researched, activates pathways that protect mitochondria, suppress chronic inflammation, and maintain insulin sensitivity. SIRT3 manages mitochondrial energy production directly. Together, sirtuins function as master regulators of the cellular aging process — and their decline after 40 is reversible with targeted NAD+ support. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the single best longevity supplement for women over 40?

There is no single best supplement because different aging mechanisms require different interventions. NMN addresses NAD+ and sirtuins; urolithin A addresses mitophagy; lifestyle practices (fasting, exercise) address autophagy. The most effective approach combines several targeted strategies.

How long does it take to see results from a longevity supplement protocol?

Measurable changes in energy and recovery typically appear within 4 to 8 weeks of consistent supplementation. Cellular-level changes in markers like NAD+ levels, mitochondrial function, or telomere attrition rates take 3 to 6 months to assess meaningfully.

Is autophagy the same as fasting?

No — fasting is one trigger for autophagy, not the same thing. Autophagy is a cellular process; fasting is one of the most reliable ways to activate it. Exercise, certain supplements, and caloric restriction also activate autophagy through overlapping mechanisms.

Can you reverse biological aging?

Current science supports slowing and partially reversing specific markers of biological aging (NAD+ levels, mitochondrial function, cellular senescence) but not reversing chronological age. The goal of longevity supplementation is to close the gap between biological and chronological age.
