Why Bloating Gets Worse After 40 and How to Address It Naturally

Bloating after 40 is not the same as the bloating you had in your 20s, and solving it requires understanding what has actually changed in your body. Hormonal shifts, microbiome changes, and declining digestive enzyme production all converge in the years around perimenopause to make bloating more frequent, more persistent, and harder to resolve with generic advice.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Post-40 bloating has specific causes distinct from bloating in younger women, including hormonal effects on gut motility.
  • Declining estrogen directly slows the movement of food through your digestive tract.
  • The gut microbiome loses diversity with age, affecting fermentation and gas production.
  • Digestive enzyme production decreases gradually after 40, making some foods harder to break down.
  • SIBO (small intestinal bacterial overgrowth) becomes more common in midlife.
  • Most generic bloating remedies address symptoms without fixing the underlying cause.

What the Research Says

Research consistently shows estrogen supports colonic transit time, and declining estrogen during menopause is associated with increased constipation and bloating. On microbiome changes, a large observational study found microbial diversity consistently declines with advancing age, with notable changes in the fourth and fifth decades.

According to Happy Aging's review, the evidence most consistently supports a combined approach: addressing gut motility through fiber and movement, supporting the microbiome with targeted probiotics and prebiotic foods, and supplementing digestive enzymes for consistent post-meal bloating.

The Happy Aging Recommendation: 6-Step Bloating Protocol

  1. Stabilize fiber intake at 25 grams per day from whole food sources. Increase by no more than 5 grams per week to avoid excess fermentation.
  2. Add a targeted multi-strain probiotic with Lactobacillus acidophilus and Bifidobacterium longum (minimum 10 billion CFU). Take consistently for at least 8 weeks.
  3. Try digestive enzyme support with meals. A broad-spectrum blend with lipase, amylase, protease, and lactase taken with your largest meal.
  4. Walk for 15 minutes after your largest meal. Post-meal movement supports gastric emptying.
  5. Reduce ultra-processed food and artificial sweeteners. Sugar alcohols (sorbitol, xylitol) are significant fermentation triggers.
  6. Rule out SIBO if symptoms are severe or persistent after 4 to 6 weeks. SIBO requires targeted medical treatment.
