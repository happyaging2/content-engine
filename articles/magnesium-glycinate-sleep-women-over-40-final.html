Magnesium Glycinate for Sleep in Women Over 40: How It Works

Magnesium glycinate supports sleep in women over 40 by binding GABA receptors in the brain and helping to lower elevated nighttime cortisol, two of the most common biochemical reasons sleep quality declines after 40.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Magnesium glycinate is magnesium bound to glycine, which crosses the blood-brain barrier more readily than other forms.
  • It activates GABA receptors, the same calming pathway targeted by sleep medications, without the dependency risk.
  • Women over 40 are more likely to be magnesium-deficient due to hormonal changes, stress, and dietary gaps.
  • A double-blind RCT (PMID 23853635) found magnesium supplementation improved sleep efficiency by 13.6% in older adults.
  • Magnesium glycinate is gentler on the digestive system than magnesium citrate or oxide.
  • It is not a sedative. It works by restoring a deficit, not forcing sleep.

Why It Matters for Women Over 40

After 40, estrogen helps the kidneys retain magnesium. As estrogen declines, magnesium excretion increases. Chronic stress depletes magnesium further. Magnesium also plays a direct role in the GABA system and regulates melatonin production through its role in pineal gland enzyme activity. Glycine, the carrier amino acid, independently lowers core body temperature, a key physiological trigger for sleep onset.

The Happy Aging Recommendation

  1. Take magnesium glycinate 30-60 minutes before bed.
  2. Start with 200 mg elemental magnesium as glycinate. Increase to 300-400 mg after one week if needed.
  3. Take it every night for 3-4 weeks before evaluating results.
  4. Pair with dim light: avoid screens 60 minutes before bed.
  5. Take antibiotics, thyroid medication, or bisphosphonates at least 2 hours apart.
