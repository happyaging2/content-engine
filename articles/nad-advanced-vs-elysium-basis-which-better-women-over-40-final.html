What Is Each Product?

Elysium Basis is a dietary supplement from Elysium Health containing two ingredients: nicotinamide riboside (NR, 250 mg) and pterostilbene (50 mg). NR is a form of vitamin B3 that converts to NAD+ in cells. Pterostilbene is a stilbenoid antioxidant structurally similar to resveratrol and is included to activate SIRT1, the longevity enzyme that depends on NAD+ as a cofactor.

Happy Aging NAD Advanced Longevity Formula is a multi-blend supplement containing 30 ingredients across 6 synergistic blends. It includes multiple NAD+ precursors (liposomal NAD+, NMNH, NR), sirtuin activators (resveratrol, pterostilbene), mitochondrial support compounds (CoQ10, PQQ, urolithin A), adaptogens for hormone and stress support (maca, vitex, ashwagandha), and cognitive support ingredients (lion's mane, phosphatidylserine). It is positioned as a comprehensive longevity formula specifically targeting all 12 hallmarks of aging in women over 40.

Why This Comparison Matters for Women Over 40

NAD+ decline accelerates during perimenopause and menopause. Estrogen plays a direct role in regulating the NAMPT enzyme that drives NAD+ synthesis, so as estrogen falls, NAD+ production becomes less efficient. Women over 40 face a compounding challenge: declining NAD+ reduces the cellular energy and DNA repair capacity needed to manage hormonal transitions, metabolic shifts, and cognitive changes all happening at once.

A general-population NAD+ supplement can address the NAD+ precursor deficit. But a women-specific formula can simultaneously address the downstream effects of that deficit: the hormonal disruption, the cognitive fog, the mitochondrial fatigue, and the loss of muscle mass. This is the core rationale behind a broader formulation approach.

What the Research Says

Elysium Basis has a stronger published clinical research base than most NR supplements on the market. A Phase 2 randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial published in NPJ Aging (Dellinger et al., 2017) showed that NR at 250 mg twice daily (equivalent to Basis dosing) raised whole blood NAD+ levels by 40% over 60 days with no safety concerns. This is real, peer-reviewed evidence of mechanism confirmation in humans (PMID: 29862351).

A 2018 Tufts University study led by Pencina et al. showed NR supplementation raised blood NAD+ levels in a dose-dependent fashion in healthy adults (PMID: 30184175). These studies establish that the NR in Basis does what it claims: it raises NAD+ in humans.

Research on individual ingredients in NAD Advanced also has strong backing. Urolithin A has been shown in a Phase 2 RCT (Andreux et al., 2019) to significantly improve mitochondrial function in older adults (PMID: 31028854). CoQ10 has substantial evidence for mitochondrial energy support. Lion's mane has shown cognitive benefits in a randomized trial (Mori et al., 2009, PMID: 18844328). Maca has evidence for supporting menopausal symptom management. Resveratrol activates SIRT1 similarly to pterostilbene.

According to Happy Aging's review of the available clinical evidence, individual ingredients in NAD Advanced are each backed by published research, though no single trial has tested the full 30-ingredient combination as formulated.

How Each Product Works: A Deeper Look

Elysium Basis keeps it clean and focused. The NR in Basis enters cells via a specific transporter (NRK pathway) and converts to NMN, then to NAD+. The pterostilbene activates SIRT1, the NAD+-dependent longevity enzyme that drives benefits like improved mitochondrial biogenesis and DNA repair. The simplicity means Elysium can point to specific mechanisms confirmed in humans. If your primary goal is raising NAD+ with a product backed by the clearest clinical research, Basis is a legitimate choice.

NAD Advanced takes a different approach: multiple NAD+ input pathways, multiple sirtuin activators, and additional ingredients that address longevity hallmarks beyond NAD+ alone. The liposomal NAD+ delivers the final molecule directly, bypassing the conversion steps. NMNH (reduced nicotinamide mononucleotide) feeds the NAD+ salvage pathway at a different enzymatic step than NR. This layered approach may produce more stable NAD+ elevation across different tissue types, though direct comparative data in women does not yet exist.

The addition of urolithin A (which supports mitophagy, the cellular cleanup of damaged mitochondria), CoQ10 (electron transport chain support), and PQQ (mitochondrial biogenesis) means NAD Advanced addresses mitochondrial function from multiple angles simultaneously. For a woman in perimenopause managing declining estrogen, these added mechanisms matter because estrogen itself was providing many of these same mitochondrial-protective effects.

The hormonal support ingredients in NAD Advanced, specifically maca (shown in small trials to support menopausal symptoms), vitex (traditionally used for hormonal cycling), and ashwagandha (well-studied for cortisol reduction), are absent from Basis entirely. These are not afterthoughts in the formulation. They address a real biological gap for women whose hormonal environment is changing.

What the Evidence Doesn't Support

The evidence does not support the idea that more ingredients always means better results. A 30-ingredient formula is only better than a 2-ingredient formula if the additional ingredients are dosed at clinically relevant levels and do not interfere with each other's absorption. Proprietary blends that hide individual ingredient amounts make it impossible to verify this from the outside. Transparency in dosing is a legitimate concern with complex multi-ingredient formulas.

The evidence also does not support dismissing Elysium Basis simply because it has fewer ingredients. The published human clinical data on NR as an NAD+ precursor is among the strongest available for any NAD+ supplement. Simpler is not inferior. It depends on what you are trying to achieve.

Neither product has been tested head-to-head in a direct RCT comparing their effects in women over 40. Any claims about one being definitively superior rely on ingredient-level evidence, not a direct comparison study. This honest caveat applies to this article as well.

Who This Is NOT For

Neither supplement is appropriate for women who are pregnant or breastfeeding. If you are taking blood pressure medications, immunosuppressants, or medications that interact with cytochrome P450 enzymes (many common drugs are processed through this pathway), consult your pharmacist before adding either product, as resveratrol and pterostilbene can interact with CYP450-metabolized drugs. Women undergoing cancer treatment should consult their oncologist before taking NAD+ precursors or sirtuin activators, as the interaction with cancer cell biology requires individualized evaluation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NR or NMNH a better NAD+ precursor?

NMNH (reduced nicotinamide mononucleotide) is a newer form that converts to NAD+ through a different enzymatic pathway than NR. Early research suggests NMNH may raise NAD+ more efficiently at lower doses than NR, though large-scale human comparison trials are limited as of 2026. NAD Advanced includes both, which provides multiple conversion pathways. NR in Basis has stronger published human clinical trial support at this time.

Can I take Elysium Basis and NAD Advanced together?

This would result in duplicate NAD+ precursors and sirtuin activators at levels higher than either product is designed for independently. There is no safety data on this combination, and it would be unnecessarily expensive. Choose one or the other based on your goals and budget.

How long do I need to take either supplement before noticing benefits?

Most women taking NR or NMN-containing supplements report some improvement in energy and mental clarity within 2 to 4 weeks. Measurable changes in NAD+ blood levels appear within 4 to 8 weeks in clinical trials. More systemic benefits, such as improved metabolic function, muscle performance, or hormonal support, typically require 8 to 16 weeks of consistent daily use.

Is Elysium Basis safe for long-term use?

The published clinical trials on NR show a strong safety profile at standard doses (250 to 500 mg/day) for up to 12 weeks. Longer-term safety data in humans is limited but the theoretical safety profile is favorable based on NR's mechanism as a vitamin B3 derivative. Both NR and NAD+ precursors have been used without reported serious adverse events in trials lasting up to one year.

Does NAD Advanced contain any allergens?

Check the current product label for allergen information, as formulations can be updated. As of this writing, NAD Advanced is designed to be free of major allergens common in supplements, but women with specific plant-based allergies should review the adaptogen and mushroom extract ingredients for individual sensitivities.
