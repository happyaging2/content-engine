Digestive Enzymes for Women Over 40: Do They Actually Help?

Digestive enzymes do help for some women over 40, but only if enzyme decline is actually part of what is causing your symptoms. Subclinical enzyme decline is genuinely common in midlife and targeted supplementation can meaningfully reduce meal-triggered bloating, gas, and discomfort, but most over-the-counter enzyme products are underdosed and poorly matched to what each person actually needs.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Digestive enzyme production naturally declines with age.
  • Different enzymes break down different macronutrients: lactase for dairy, amylase for carbohydrates, lipase for fats, protease for protein.
  • Subclinical enzyme decline means producing fewer enzymes than optimal, not zero.
  • Most OTC enzyme supplements contain lower doses than what research uses in clinical trials.
  • Enzyme supplements work best when taken immediately before or with meals.

What the Research Says

A randomized controlled trial examining a broad-spectrum digestive enzyme blend found statistically significant reductions in post-meal symptom scores compared to placebo over 8 weeks. For lactase specifically, multiple well-designed trials confirm that lactase enzyme supplements taken with dairy-containing meals effectively reduce lactose intolerance symptoms.

According to Happy Aging's review, the evidence supports a trial of broad-spectrum enzyme supplementation for women experiencing consistent post-meal bloating, particularly those who have noticed increasing food intolerances in midlife.

The Happy Aging Recommendation: 5-Step Enzyme Approach

  1. Identify your symptom pattern. Dairy bloating points to lactase deficiency. Fat-heavy meal bloating points to lipase decline. Carbohydrate bloating points to amylase.
  2. Read the enzyme activity units, not just milligrams. Enzymes are dosed in FIP, DU, HUT, and FCC units. Compare to the dose ranges in the table above.
  3. Take enzymes immediately before or at the start of meals. Enzymes taken after a meal have significantly reduced effectiveness.
  4. Trial consistently for 4 weeks before evaluating results.
  5. Combine with a probiotic for comprehensive gut support. Enzymes and probiotics address different aspects of gut function and work well together.
