GLP-1 Medications and Bone Loss: What Women Over 40 Should Know

GLP-1 receptor agonist medications like semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy) and tirzepatide (Mounjaro) are delivering remarkable weight loss results, but rapid weight loss accelerates bone resorption, and women over 40 are already at elevated risk for bone density decline.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Rapid weight loss from GLP-1 medications is associated with accelerated bone mineral density loss.
  • Women over 40 are already losing bone density at a rate that accelerates sharply at perimenopause.
  • Key protective nutrients include calcium, vitamin D3 with K2, magnesium, and adequate protein.
  • Resistance training is the most evidence-supported behavioral intervention for preserving bone during weight loss.
  • GLP-1 medications' appetite suppression makes hitting micronutrient targets through food alone very difficult.

The Happy Aging Recommendation

  1. Start your bone protection protocol immediately from week one of GLP-1 therapy.
  2. Get a baseline DEXA scan if you are 45 or older.
  3. Begin resistance training within the first two weeks.
  4. Track your protein intake. Use a food tracking app or high-quality protein supplement.
  5. Test vitamin D and adjust supplementation accordingly.
  6. Re-test bone density at 12 months.
