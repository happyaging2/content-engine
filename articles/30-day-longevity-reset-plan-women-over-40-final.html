What Is a Longevity Reset?

A longevity reset is a structured, time-bounded protocol designed to establish the foundational behaviors and inputs that longevity science identifies as most impactful for biological aging in a specific population. For women over 40, this means addressing the specific physiological changes that accumulate through perimenopause and beyond: declining NAD+ levels affecting cellular energy, sleep architecture changes from hormonal shifts, gut microbiome alterations that affect immunity and mood, elevated baseline stress and cortisol, and loss of muscle mass from declining activity and protein synthesis efficiency.

The "reset" framing is intentional. Thirty days is long enough to establish new patterns at the cellular, microbiome, and behavioral level, and short enough to stay engaged with a defined endpoint. It is a commitment with a measurable beginning and middle, not an open-ended lifestyle overhaul that asks for indefinite willpower.

Why This Matters After 40

The women's longevity research of the past decade has moved from a disease-prevention model toward a vitality-optimization model. The question is no longer just "how do we prevent illness?" but "how do we maintain and even improve function, energy, cognitive clarity, and physical resilience through and beyond menopause?" The answer emerging from research involves multiple simultaneous interventions rather than single-target approaches.

The Five Longevity Pillars

Each pillar is supported by its own body of evidence and addresses a distinct physiological system. The synergy between pillars is what makes simultaneous implementation more powerful than sequential addressing of each.

Pillar 1: Cellular Energy (NAD+). NAD+ is a coenzyme central to mitochondrial function, DNA repair, and cellular stress response. Research indicates NAD+ levels decline with age, beginning meaningfully in the mid-30s and accelerating through the 40s and 50s. Rajman, Chwalek, and Sinclair (2018, Cell Metabolism, PMID 29502867) reviewed the in vivo evidence for NAD+-boosting molecules, finding that NAD+ precursor supplementation supports cellular energy metabolism and activates longevity-associated pathways including sirtuins. Yoshino et al. (2021, Science, PMID 34811282) conducted a randomized controlled trial of NMN supplementation in 25 prediabetic women, finding improvements in muscle insulin sensitivity over 10 weeks of 300mg per day dosing.

Pillar 2: Sleep Quality. Sleep is when cellular repair, memory consolidation, hormonal regulation, and immune function are most active. Poor sleep in women over 40, driven largely by estrogen and progesterone decline affecting sleep architecture, accelerates multiple aging pathways simultaneously. Addressing sleep is not optional in a longevity protocol because poor sleep blunts the benefits of every other pillar. A randomized controlled trial (Abbasi et al., 2012, PMID 23853635) found magnesium supplementation improved sleep efficiency by 13.6% versus placebo in 46 older adults, providing a practical and accessible entry point for sleep support.

Pillar 3: Stress Regulation. Chronic psychological and physiological stress elevates cortisol, which suppresses NAD+ utilization, disrupts sleep, promotes inflammatory signaling, impairs gut barrier function, and directly accelerates cellular aging. Stress regulation is the pillar that women over 40 most commonly deprioritize, partly because the strategies (meditation, breathing, nature exposure) feel less "medical" than supplements or exercise. The evidence, however, is consistent: regular stress-reduction practices reduce cortisol and inflammatory markers in ways that directly support the other four pillars.

Pillar 4: Gut Health. The gut microbiome shifts significantly through menopause, with reduced diversity and loss of key bacteria affecting immune regulation, mood neurotransmitter production, estrogen metabolism, and inflammatory tone. Addressing gut health through dietary fiber, fermented foods, and targeted probiotic support directly supports immune resilience and the microbial environment that influences every other pillar.

Pillar 5: Movement. Regular physical activity is the most consistently evidence-supported longevity intervention across all populations. For women over 40, two movement types are most critical: Zone 2 cardio (low-intensity sustained aerobic exercise at approximately 60-70% maximum heart rate) for mitochondrial health and metabolic efficiency, and resistance training for muscle mass preservation and bone density. Zone 2 cardio in particular supports mitochondrial biogenesis, which directly complements NAD+ supplementation at the cellular energy pillar.

The 30-Day Longevity Reset Protocol

Week 1: Foundation

  1. Establish a fixed wake time. Choose a realistic wake time and commit to it for 7 days, including weekends.
  2. Start NAD+ supplementation. Begin NAD Advanced Longevity Formula with breakfast each morning.
  3. Set a protein target of 25-30g per meal.
  4. Begin Zone 2 cardio 3 times this week.
  5. Start magnesium glycinate before bed. Take 300-400mg approximately 1 hour before your target sleep time.
  6. Take a 10-minute post-dinner walk each evening.
  7. End of Week 1 check-in: Note your baseline energy (1-10), sleep quality (1-10), and any digestive symptoms.

Week 2: Gut Reset

  1. Eliminate the top 3 inflammatory inputs for 14 days.
  2. Target 25g or more of dietary fiber per day.
  3. Start the Happiest Gut probiotic 15-30 minutes before breakfast.
  4. Add 2 servings of fermented foods this week.
  5. Track energy and sleep quality daily.

Week 3: Cellular Support

  1. Add a daily stress-reduction practice for the remaining 14 days of the protocol.
  2. Optimize supplement timing using the morning and evening structure.
  3. Add resistance training twice this week.
  4. Sleep environment audit.
  5. End of Week 3 check-in.

Week 4: Integration and Assessment

  1. Add one new longevity habit.
  2. Conduct a full protocol review.
  3. Review energy, sleep, mood, and digestive function against Week 1 benchmarks.
  4. Plan your Month 2 protocol.
