Estrogen decline is a primary driver of the sleep disruption women experience after 40, because estrogen supports the serotonin-to-melatonin conversion pathway and promotes the deep, restorative slow-wave sleep (N3) that becomes harder to sustain as hormone levels fall.

What to Know

  • Sleep architecture refers to the cycling pattern of sleep stages, and estrogen decline specifically reduces time in N3 deep slow-wave sleep.
  • Estrogen supports serotonin production, which converts to melatonin.
  • Progesterone has a GABA-like calming effect on the brain. Its decline in perimenopause increases middle-of-the-night waking.
  • A randomized controlled trial (PMID 23853635) found magnesium supplementation improved sleep efficiency by 13.6% versus placebo.

What Is Sleep Architecture?

Sleep architecture is the structured pattern of sleep stages: N1 (Light Sleep), N2 (Stable Sleep), N3 (Deep Slow-Wave Sleep), and REM (Rapid Eye Movement).

What the Research Says
