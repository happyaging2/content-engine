What Are These Products?

NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell in your body. It is essential for energy production, DNA repair, and the activity of sirtuins, the enzymes most associated with longevity research. NAD+ levels decline significantly with age, and supplementing with NAD+ precursors is one of the most-studied approaches to supporting cellular health in aging adults.

Tru Niagen is made by ChromaDex and contains nicotinamide riboside (NR), a specific form of vitamin B3 that converts to NAD+ in cells. NR has more published human clinical trials than almost any other NAD+ precursor. Tru Niagen is a single-ingredient, dose-controlled product.

Happy Aging NAD+ Women's Longevity Formula is a 30-ingredient comprehensive longevity formula built around advanced NAD+ precursors (including NMNH, also known as reduced NMN, and liposomal NAD+) alongside ingredients targeting mitochondrial function, cellular senescence, hormonal support, and DNA repair. It was co-developed with Dr. Daniel Yadegar, a Harvard-trained longevity cardiologist, specifically for women over 40.

Why It Matters for Women Over 40

NAD+ is not the only thing that declines with age. Hormonal changes, mitochondrial dysfunction, accumulation of senescent cells, and reduced autophagy all occur simultaneously in women over 40. A formula designed to address only one of these pathways may boost NAD+ levels while leaving other aging mechanisms unaddressed.

Recent research through 2026 suggests that the hallmarks of aging interact with each other. For example, low NAD+ impairs sirtuin function, which reduces the cell's ability to clear senescent cells, which increases inflammation, which further depletes NAD+.

Happy Aging's position: for women over 40 who want comprehensive longevity support across multiple systems, a 30-ingredient women-specific formula addresses more of the biological aging picture than a single NAD+ precursor alone.

What the Research Says

According to Happy Aging's review of over 30 studies on NAD+ precursors, the NR research base (which directly applies to Tru Niagen) is genuinely strong. Multiple randomized controlled trials confirm that oral NR supplementation raises whole-blood NAD+ levels in humans.

NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide), the parent compound of NMNH used in Happy Aging's formula, also has a growing human trial base. NMNH specifically is a newer form that appears to raise NAD+ more efficiently per gram than NMN in early research. Liposomal delivery is supported by pharmacokinetic research suggesting improved cellular uptake.

The additional ingredients in Happy Aging's formula, including resveratrol, pterostilbene, spermidine, urolithin A, and the hormonal support blend, each have their own research base.

What the Evidence Does Not Support

Neither product should be presented as a substitute for lifestyle fundamentals. NAD+ supplementation research is promising but not yet at the stage where clinical trials have confirmed dramatic longevity extension in humans. Multi-ingredient formulas have inherent complexity in attributing effects to individual components.
