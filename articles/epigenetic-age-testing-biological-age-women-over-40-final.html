Epigenetic age tests measure DNA methylation patterns in your cells to estimate your biological age, which can be meaningfully younger or older than your chronological age depending on how you have lived.

You may have heard the phrase "you are only as old as you feel," but science is increasingly able to put a number on biological aging. Epigenetic clocks, a category of laboratory tests that read chemical marks on your DNA, can now estimate how fast your cells are aging independently of your birthday. As of 2026, these tools have moved from academic research laboratories to direct-to-consumer products, and women over 40 are among the most interested users.

What to Know

  • Epigenetic age tests analyze DNA methylation patterns to estimate biological age, which may differ from your chronological age by years in either direction.
  • The most studied epigenetic clocks include the Horvath clock, Hannum clock, PhenoAge, and GrimAge.
  • Lifestyle factors including exercise, quality sleep, diet quality, stress reduction, and not smoking are consistently associated with younger biological age scores.
  • Epigenetic age tests are wellness tools, not medical diagnostics.

What Is Epigenetic Age Testing?

Epigenetic age testing reads DNA methylation patterns from a biological sample and uses these patterns to estimate how old your cells appear to be, biologically speaking.
