## 2. Featured Snippet Answer
After 40, metabolism slows primarily because of muscle loss — the body loses 3–8% of muscle mass per decade. Less muscle means fewer calories burned at rest. Declining estrogen also shifts fat storage to the abdomen, and falling NAD+ levels reduce cellular energy production efficiency.
