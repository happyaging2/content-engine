Happy Aging Glow Shot vs Vital Proteins Collagen Peptides: Which Is Better for Women Over 40?

Both Happy Aging Glow Shot and Vital Proteins Collagen Peptides are legitimate collagen supplements with real collagen content, but they differ in source, collagen type, additional ingredients, and how they are formulated for specific outcomes. For women over 40 focused primarily on skin health, Glow Shot's marine Type I collagen focus gives it a targeted advantage, while Vital Proteins remains one of the most widely available and trusted collagen brands for general connective tissue support.

What to Know Before You Read
  • Happy Aging Glow Shot uses marine collagen (from fish), which is primarily Type I collagen.
  • Vital Proteins Collagen Peptides uses bovine collagen (from cows), which provides Type I and Type III collagen.
  • Type I collagen is the dominant structural collagen in skin. Both products deliver it.
  • Marine collagen has slightly smaller peptide size, which may support bioavailability.
  • Vital Proteins is widely available at retail, well-priced, and has a large evidence base.
  • Glow Shot is formulated specifically for women over 40 with additional skin health ingredients.

What the Research Says

A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial found women taking 2.5 grams of hydrolyzed marine collagen daily for 12 weeks showed a 28% improvement in skin hydration versus 9% in the placebo group. Bovine collagen research at 10 grams per day also shows significant skin outcomes. The key difference is dose: marine collagen appears to produce skin outcomes at lower doses (2.5-5 grams) compared to most bovine collagen skin trials (10+ grams).

According to Happy Aging's review, no single large-scale RCT has directly compared marine and bovine collagen head-to-head for skin outcomes in women over 40. The evidence that marine collagen is superior for skin is based on the weight of source-specific trials, bioavailability research, and the Type I concentration argument.

The Happy Aging Recommendation

  1. Choose based on your primary goal: skin focus points to marine Type I; broader connective tissue goals are served well by bovine Type I and III.
  2. Use consistently for at least 8 to 12 weeks before evaluating skin results.
  3. Take with adequate vitamin C (75-120 mg per day), which is required for collagen synthesis.
  4. Protect your skin from UV damage daily with SPF 30 or higher.
  5. Pair with adequate total protein intake (0.8-1.2 g per kg body weight daily).
